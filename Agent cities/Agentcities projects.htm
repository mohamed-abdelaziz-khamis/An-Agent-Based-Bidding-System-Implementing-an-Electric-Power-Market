About Agentcities

Overview

The development of a large scale dynamic service environment such as the one envisaged by Agentcities requires many technologies, has many facets and is only attainable through the collaborative efforts of many people. The overiding objective of Agentcities is therefore to use the developing network as a focal which enables all that can be used to:

  • Validate research and commercial technology results in an open environment.
  • Enable different groups around the world to link up their systems.
  • Ensure global participation and equal access to the effort - facilitating long-term take up of results in global commerce.
One of the best ways of doing this is by establishing links between research projects around the world.

    Area Australia
    Name Flexible and robust protocol-based interaction between agents in open systems
    Start Date March 2002
    Funding Agency ARC Linkage grant (2002-2004)
    Partners RMIT University (Computer Science Department) and Agent Oriented Software
    Duration 3 years
    Topic Areas The project aims to explore robustness, extensibility and flexibility of interaction protocols in open systems. It plans to use AgentCities as a testbed for implementation and exploration of ideas. We are interested amongst other things in how to make interaction protocols more robust in the face of various failures, how to transfer/accept protocols, and development of extensible representations of protocols.
    Contact/URL http://www.cs.rmit.edu.au/agents/protocols
